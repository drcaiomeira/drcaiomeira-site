Augmentation rhinoplasty: definition and support while respecting identity and facial proportions

For many Black and Asian noses, the priority is augmentation and definition with stable support—not only reduction. Planning considers skin thickness, cartilage support, nasal base, and facial proportions to achieve a natural, long-term result.

Why it’s different

Black and Asian noses often have distinct structural characteristics (skin thickness, cartilage support, and nasal base). “Standard reduction-only” approaches can look unnatural or be unstable.

How I evaluate

Facial proportions, skin thickness, projection, tip support, nasal base, symmetry, and goals. The focus is shape with stable support.

Grafts & structure

In selected cases, grafts help create augmentation and definition with stability. I may use septal cartilage and, when indicated, rib cartilage for structural support.

Who benefits most

  • Patients seeking more projection/definition while staying natural
  • Those who value stability over “instant” results
  • Cases where augmentation is more appropriate than reduction

Expectations & timeline

Healing is gradual. With thicker skin, tip refinement may take longer. The goal is a harmonious and stable long-term outcome.
