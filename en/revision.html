Revision rhinoplasty: reconstruction, support, and predictability

Revision requires a detailed analysis of prior changes and what must be rebuilt. The goal is to restore support, symmetry, and proportion with a realistic and safe plan.

Common concerns

  • Weak/unsupported tip
  • Persistent asymmetry
  • Nostril retraction
  • Irregular dorsum / collapse

Why revision is different

  • Scar tissue and altered anatomy
  • Limited septal cartilage
  • Higher need for structural grafts
  • More conservative, precise planning

Grafts (when indicated)

  • Septal cartilage
  • Rib cartilage for reconstruction
  • Structured support and contour

Consultation

I review history, examine the nose, assess photos and, when needed, functional aspects. We discuss what can be improved, anatomical limits, and risks transparently.

Expectation management is essential in revision surgery.
