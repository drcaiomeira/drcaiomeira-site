Structured technique

Rhinoplasty with a focus on structure, function, and natural aesthetics

The goal is predictable support and natural proportions. In selected cases, I use septal cartilage and, when reconstruction or augmentation is needed, rib cartilage.

Goals

  • Natural look (no “operated” appearance)
  • Tip and dorsum support
  • Facial harmony
  • Long-term stability

Male rhinoplasty

  • Male-specific proportions and lines
  • Control of tip position and rotation
  • Avoid feminization

Augmentation (Black & Asian noses)

  • Augmentation of dorsum/tip when indicated
  • Respect for identity and facial proportions
  • Support-first planning

Evaluation

I evaluate aesthetic and functional concerns, asymmetries, skin thickness, cartilage support, and surgical history. The plan is individualized to increase predictability and reduce late structural issues.

What to expect

Healing is gradual. Swelling and refinement vary with skin thickness and case complexity. Setting realistic expectations is part of structured planning.
