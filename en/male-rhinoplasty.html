Male rhinoplasty: preserve identity, avoid feminization, and build long-term stability

Male rhinoplasty requires different planning. The goal is not to “thin” the nose, but to create harmony while preserving masculine proportions. My approach is structured, prioritizing support and natural aesthetics— so the nose does not look operated.

What often goes wrong

Many men avoid surgery due to fear of feminization. This can happen when female aesthetic standards are applied to a male face.

How I plan

I evaluate facial proportions, skin thickness, cartilage support, and your goals. The plan is designed to preserve identity and avoid exaggerated changes.

Structured technique

Support and control are the priority. In selected cases, I use septal cartilage and structural grafts when indicated to improve stability and predictability.

Common indications

  • Dorsal hump, irregularities, or asymmetry
  • Nasal deviation with functional concerns
  • Dropped or disproportionate tip
  • Post-traumatic deformity
  • Goal: natural, masculine outcome

Expectations & timeline

Healing is gradual. Men with thicker skin may have a slower tip refinement. Stability and final appearance mature over time.
